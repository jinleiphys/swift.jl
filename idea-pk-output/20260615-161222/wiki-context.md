## Read entries (papers / methods / systems already in the user's wiki on this topic)

- [[2011-prc-lazauskas-carbonell-cs-few-body-scattering]] — **THE benchmark paper itself, archived in the user's wiki.** It gives the EXACT amplitude-extraction formulas the swift.jl reproduction must match:
  - Inhomogeneous form Ψ = Ψ^in + Ψ^sc; under r→r e^{iθ} the outgoing part decays exp(−kr sinθ)/r so V·Ψ^in is the source of a bound-state-like problem (this is what swift does).
  - TWO routes to the 3-body scattered amplitude: (Eq. 16) **orthogonal-channel projection** — read the coefficient of the bound-pair × outgoing-wave at the y-asymptote: f_{nm}(ŷ) = C_n^{-1}|y| exp(−i q_n y e^{iθ}) ∫ φ_n*(x e^{-iθ}) ψ̄^sc(x,y) e^{3iθ} d³x ; OR (Eq. 17+19) **Green's-theorem integral** on bound×scattered overlap: f = −C_n^{-1}(m/ħ²) ∫∫ φ_n*(x e^{-iθ}) [V_j(x_j e^{iθ}) + V_k(x_k e^{iθ})] Ψ̄(x,y) e^{6iθ} d³x d³y. Green-theorem version ~1 digit more accurate.
  - NOTE the pieces swift.jl's `f = -4μ/(ħ²k²)·⟨φ|V·Rxy_31|ψ_total⟩` may be MISSING vs Eq.17: the deuteron normalization coefficient **C_n^{-1}** (C_n = ∫ φ_n*(x e^{-iθ}) φ_n(x e^{iθ}) e^{3iθ} d³x), the **CS Jacobian factors e^{3iθ}/e^{6iθ}**, and using the OTHER two pair potentials **[V_j+V_k]** (not V_i of the incoming pair) — the Rxy rearrangement is how those enter. Wrong/missing C_n or Jacobian → wrong |S| magnitude (exactly the η≈1 vs 0.4649 symptom).
  - Two-body warm-up (Table I): NN ¹S₀ at E_cm=1 MeV reproduces exact δ=63.512° (MT only) / 50.491° (MT+Coulomb) to 5 digits, r_max=100, θ=10°. → an ISOLATED test of the amplitude/phase normalization with no recoupling/deuteron/breakup.
  - Eq.(18) θ_max convergence bound: 14.2° @14.1 MeV, 8.9° @42 MeV. Discretization: 30–40 splines/direction for 3 digits.
  - n-d phase plateaus in θ∈[4°,12.5°] ABOVE breakup (Fig.3). Inelasticity-η and breakup amplitude are tabulated (Table V, ³S₁ breakup at 42 MeV vs Witała to 3 digits).

CS-FY n-³H above breakup agrees with Deltuva complex-energy AGS to ~3 digits; "largest discrepancy 0.5% in ¹S₀ η NEAR UNITY" — i.e. η near 1 is the hard regime, but their η is correct because the extraction is right. - [[methods/integral-relations]] (Kievsky-Viviani) — ALTERNATIVE extraction the user has fully noted: tan δ = B/A, B=−(m/ħ²)⟨Ψ|H−E|F⟩, A=(m/ħ²)⟨Ψ|H−E|G_reg⟩, with F regular and G_reg=G(1−e^{−γr}) regularized irregular free solutions. Works from INTERIOR (square-integrable) Ψ; asymptotics need not be physical. Papers: Barletta PRL 2009, Kievsky PRC 81 034002 (2010, canonical derivation, n-d/p-d), Romero-Redondo PRA 2011 (multichannel, all partial waves), Garrido PRA 2012 (above breakup).
